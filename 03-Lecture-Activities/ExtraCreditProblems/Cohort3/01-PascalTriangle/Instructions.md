**Title: Pascal's Triangle II**

**Problem Statement:**
Given an integer `rowIndex`, return the `rowIndex`-th (0-indexed) row of Pascal's triangle.

**Additional Details:**
- Pascal's triangle is a triangular array of the binomial coefficients. Each number is the sum of the two numbers directly above it.
- In Pascal's triangle, the first and last numbers of each row are always 1.

**Example 1:**
- Input: `rowIndex = 3`
- Output: `[1, 3, 3, 1]`

**Example 2:**
- Input: `rowIndex = 0`
- Output: `[1]`

**Example 3:**
- Input: `rowIndex = 1`
- Output: `[1, 1]`

**Constraints:**
- `0 <= rowIndex <= 33`


---

Sample Pascal's Triangle:
     1
    1 1
   1 2 1
  1 3 3 1
 1 4 6 4 1
1 5 10 10 5 1
